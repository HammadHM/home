History

History of the development!

The CPU has gone through vast arrays of technical achievements,
CPU's nowadays are much faster with more cores and clock speed.
Here is a brief summary of the history:

Basic Concept - 1823 and 1903

Pretty big gap of years, however the two fundamental concepts to create the modern CPU
happened in these two years. Firstly in 1823, Baron Jons Jackob Berzelius discovered
slicon, which as we know, is one of the most important material required to make a CPU.
Also, in 1903, the famous Nikola Tesla patented his electrical logic circuit, which is the
essential basis of the CPU.

A picture of slicon metal

Discovery of Transistors - 1947 to 1960

At Bell Labatories, Willam Shockley, Walter Brattain and John Bardeen created the first
transistor, which amplifies or switch electrical signal, or power. The trio later
patented their product and each got a Nobel Peace Prize.

An illistration from
The Free Dictionary

However, in 1958, the first integrated circuit was made by Robert Noyce and Jack Kilby,
an integrated circuit contains many logic gates, electrical pathways, and transistors.
IBM later created a mass-production facility for transistors in 1960.

Prime Time - 1968 to 1972

Intel was created by Robert Noyce & Gordon Moore in 1968 to become one of today's 2nd biggest
semi-conductor company, and a year later Intel's competiton Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) was
created too.

In 1971, Intel introduces the Intel 4004, which was the first microprocessor! The 4004
started at $200 and included near 2,300 transistors, 640 bytes of memory and performed about
60,000 operations per second.

Picture of 4004's circuit, by ExtremeTech

With New technology...

Clock speed and Core counts have been increased, with that - CPU can process much quicker, be much smaller,
contain more transistors and run more reliably.
