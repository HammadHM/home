Usually sighted as the brain of the PC, the CPU has three main parts that each have individual tasks to keep the computer running properly.

Here are the three parts:

  • Control Unit
  • Arithmetic Logic Unit
  • Cache

Control Units

The Control Unit or also known as CU is what commands the operations of the CPU and indicates how to respond to the instructions. It follows the 3 step plan. Fetch, decode, execute. The CPU fetches data from the memory, then decodes the data for what it becomes signals that can be sent to other components to execute the CPU's data.

Arithmetic Logic Unit

The Arithmetic Logic Unit also known as ALU is what performs the bit-shifting, logical and arithmetic calculations, it follows the Control Units request.
The ALU decodes and executes the data requested, making it a substanial part of the Central Processing Unit.

Category of Component

The CPU can be classified as a Direct Data Entry input, output, processing, and storage component.

Subsequently, CPU has to manage various components; which will require to input signals to alert other components to perform their tasks.
The CPU doesn't always require human interactions to do its tasks making it an DDE input component. Also, the executing, and outputing makes it an output component.

The CPU is also an processing component, as said by the name, and the CPU can be considered a storage device due to its cache memory.

How does it work and what materials are used

Refer to the "Functionalites" Section to see how it functions

Essentially, the CPU fetches information from the memory, then is decoded to be processed,
and then finally is executed and outputed.

The CPU requires some hefty materials, which is why the CPU is one of the most expensive component in a PC.
CPUs requires 99.99% pure silicon crystal, with doping materials - this is due to silicons easy-to-manipulate
conductive properties.
Tiny amounts of gold and copper are used to conduct electricity pathways.
CPU's also use epoxy resins and fiber glass back board.
